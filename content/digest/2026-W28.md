# Field Digest — Week 28, 2026 (w/c 6 July)

The weekly read on where People Advisory work is being disrupted, what clients are asking for now, and how organizations need to adapt. Compiled from the week's sourced signals; every claim linked.

## Most disrupted field this week: job architecture

The clearest pattern of the week is that **role structures are breaking before processes do**. Deloitte US eliminated traditional job titles outright (effective June, explicitly because AI absorbed junior work — [Future of Consulting](https://futureofconsulting.ai/ai-leadership/2026-consultings-ai-revolution-update/)), and Deloitte's own [2026 Human Capital Trends](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/talent/human-capital-trends.html) now says roles, skills, and career paths should be **rebuilt rather than adjusted** — with skills ontologies coupled to task analysis (the Cleveland Clinic pattern) as the emerging method. [PwC's AI Jobs Barometer](https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/services/ai/ai-jobs-barometer.html) adds the direction of travel: new tasks in AI-exposed roles are 2.5x more likely to demand empathy, judgement, and creativity, with social-emotional skill demand up 26% (US) / 22% (Europe) by 2030.

**Read for practitioners:** "job architecture refresh" is no longer a rewards-cycle hygiene project. It is the load-bearing wall of every agentic deployment — if agent-supervision work isn't named, graded, and pathed, the rollout stalls at the pilot gate.

## The HRIS layer is becoming agentic — fast

Three moves in one quarter: SAP shipped **15 assistants and 62 agents across SuccessFactors** with Joule and SmartRecruiters' Winston operating as connected agents ([SAP News](https://news.sap.com/2026/04/sap-successfactors-1h-2026-release/)); ServiceNow is pushing **role-scoped HR agents** on a single cross-functional platform ([AIHR Institute](https://www.aihr-institute.com/servicenows-autonomous-workforce-what-role-scoped-hr-agents-mean-for-your-hris-strategy) — single source, verify); Workday added **build/connect/monitor governance tooling** for agents touching payroll, benefits, and employee records ([TechTarget](https://www.techtarget.com/searchhrsoftware/news/366643078/How-SAP-sees-the-future-of-HR-in-the-age-of-AI) — single source, verify).

**Read:** HRIS integration engagements are quietly becoming agent-governance engagements. The client thinks they're buying an integration; what they need is decision rights, audit trails, and a works-council-ready data story.

## What clients are asking for now (the pattern shift)

1. **AI is now a default line in non-AI projects.** Reorgs, HRIS migrations, and operating-model reviews arrive with "and where do agents fit" already in the RFP. The consulting market has repriced accordingly — the ServiceNow–Accenture forward-deployed engineering program ([Accenture](https://newsroom.accenture.com/news/2026/servicenow-and-accenture-launch-forward-deployed-engineering-program-to-scale-agentic-ai-across-the-enterprise)) prices the pilot-to-production gap as a people-and-deployment problem.
2. **From "build us a pilot" to "make it stick."** With ~79% adoption but ~11% in production ([WRITER](https://writer.com/blog/enterprise-ai-adoption-2026/)) and only 25% of orgs moving 40%+ of experiments to production ([Deloitte](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/technology-management/tech-trends/2026/agentic-ai-strategy.html)), demand is shifting to adoption, governance, and value-realization work — the 80% of value that comes from work redesign, not the technology ([Deloitte HC Trends](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/talent/human-capital-trends.html)).
3. **Compliance-driven change budgets in the EU.** With the AI Act's main enforcement wave starting 2 August ([is4.ai](https://www.is4.ai/blog/our-blog-1/eu-ai-act-compliance-guide-2026-345)), Article 4 literacy programs and AI inventories are funded as compliance — smart clients are using that budget to buy real capability, not webinars.
4. **Trust engineering enters scope.** Post-Cisco ([CXM](https://cxm.world/employee-experience/cisco-ai-agent-rollout-layoffs-employee-trust/)), sequencing reviews, telemetry-use guarantees, and no-displacement commitments are appearing as explicit workstreams, especially in works-council markets.

## How an organization needs to adapt (this week's synthesis)

- Treat **job architecture as the first workstream** of any agent deployment, not the cleanup phase.
- Stand up **agent governance inside the HRIS conversation** — the platforms now ship the tooling; someone must own the decision rights.
- Use the **EU compliance clock as the forcing function** for the literacy and inventory work you should be doing anyway.
- Budget for the 80%: if the program plan spends more on licenses than on work redesign, adoption will match the industry's 11%, not the vendor deck.
