# Radar Brief — 7 July 2026

Focus: agentic AI in the enterprise, AI transformation moves by consulting firms, workforce and change-management signals.

---

## 1. Gartner: $234B of enterprise software spend at risk from agentic AI

**What happened:** Gartner's July 1 press release says up to $234 billion of enterprise application spending is exposed to "agentic arbitrage" between now and 2030 — roughly 20% of enterprise SaaS spend — as agents start doing work that seat-based software used to do.
**Why it matters for you:** This is the macro case for AI transformation consulting. Every CIO renegotiating a SaaS contract will need an operating-model answer, not just a procurement one: if agents replace seats, who owns the process, the controls, the people?
**Angle:** "Agentic arbitrage is a change-management problem wearing a procurement costume."
**Source:** [Gartner press release](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-07-01-gartner-says-us-dollars-234-billion-in-enterprise-application-software-spend-is-at-risk-from-agentic-artificial-intelligence)

## 2. Deloitte eliminates traditional job titles in the US (effective June 1)

**What happened:** Deloitte announced internally in January that traditional job titles across US divisions would be eliminated from June 1, 2026, explicitly framed as a response to AI reshaping how junior consultants work.
**Why it matters for you:** This is people advisory meeting AI head-on: career architecture, levelling, and progression models being redrawn because agents absorbed the junior workload. You have direct credibility here — you did this work at Deloitte/EY before AI forced it.
**Angle:** "When AI eats the pyramid, what replaces the title ladder?" Strong scenario-lab candidate.
**Source:** [Future of Consulting — 2026 AI revolution update](https://futureofconsulting.ai/ai-leadership/2026-consultings-ai-revolution-update/)

## 3. Cisco rolls out AI agents to every employee — the same month layoffs begin

**What happened:** Cisco is cutting under 4,000 jobs (terminations begin July 13) roughly two weeks before its AI agent reaches every remaining employee. Coverage frames it as an employee-trust collision.
**Why it matters for you:** Textbook change-management failure mode: sequencing an agent rollout against a restructuring destroys the psychological safety the rollout depends on. This is THE cautionary tale for AI adoption right now.
**Angle:** Scenario: "You're the CM lead. The CFO won't move the layoff date. What's your play?"
**Source:** [CXM — Cisco AI rollout and layoffs](https://cxm.world/employee-experience/cisco-ai-agent-rollout-layoffs-employee-trust/)

## 4. ServiceNow + Accenture launch a "forward deployed engineering" program for agentic AI

**What happened:** A joint program to put forward-deployed engineers inside enterprises to scale agentic AI past the pilot phase.
**Why it matters for you:** The market is voting that the pilot-to-production gap is a people-and-deployment problem, not a model problem. "Forward deployed" is becoming the dominant delivery model at OpenAI, Anthropic, and now the SIs — the roles you're targeting.
**Source:** [Accenture newsroom](https://newsroom.accenture.com/news/2026/servicenow-and-accenture-launch-forward-deployed-engineering-program-to-scale-agentic-ai-across-the-enterprise)

## 5. The adoption gap is the story: 79% adopted, 11% in production

**What happened:** Multiple 2026 surveys converge on the same shape: ~79% of enterprises say they've adopted AI agents, but only ~11% run them in production; a large governance gap persists. Microsoft's 2026 Work Trend Index pushes "human agency" as the framing for agent-era work design.
**Why it matters for you:** The 68-point gap between "adopted" and "in production" is precisely the change management, governance, and workforce-readiness territory you're claiming. Every post you write can hang off this number.
**Sources:** [WRITER — enterprise AI adoption 2026](https://writer.com/blog/enterprise-ai-adoption-2026/), [Microsoft Work Trend Index 2026](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/worklab/work-trend-index/agents-human-agency-and-the-opportunity-for-every-organization), [Agentic AI Institute — governance gap](https://agenticaiinstitute.org/agentic-ai-enterprise-adoption-2026-governance-gap/), [Deloitte Tech Trends 2026 — agentic reality check](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/topics/technology-management/tech-trends/2026/agentic-ai-strategy.html)

# Firm Watch — methodologies, internal processes, benchmarks

How the firms I compete with and want to join actually run AI internally, and what they publish as method.

## Methodology landscape (who has a quotable method)

Only three firms publish a numbered methodology you can quote verbatim: **BCG's 10-20-70** (10% algorithms, 20% tech/data, 70% people and process — the closest thing to a CM-first AI doctrine in the market), **McKinsey's six "Rewired" capabilities**, and **Deloitte's seven Trustworthy AI dimensions**. **KPMG** publishes the **10 Trusted AI pillars** and claims a first on **ISO/IEC 42001** (AI management system certification) — a trust taxonomy positioned as method. EY, PwC, Accenture, Bain run narrative frameworks and platforms rather than numbered steps. *(single source — verify before publishing)*
**Source:** [Consulting Huber — frameworks compared](https://consulting-huber.com/ai-consulting-frameworks-compared.html)

## Internal platforms and scale (their own transformation as the proof point)

PwC's **ChatPwC** at ~200,000 seats (reported as OpenAI's largest enterprise customer); McKinsey's **Lilli** serving 40,000+ consultants and now orchestrating ~20,000 agents; Deloitte's **Sidekick/Zora AI** at ~75,000 seats in EMEA. The firms sell the transformation they ran on themselves — their internal seat counts and agent fleets ARE the credential.
**Sources:** [Future of Consulting](https://futureofconsulting.ai/ai-leadership/2026-consultings-ai-revolution-update/), [F1GMAT Q1 2026 consulting trends](https://premium.f1gmat.com/consulting/trends/2026/Q1)

## Benchmarks worth quoting in client work

- Healthy 2026 baseline: **2-4 workflows redesigned around AI**, each with a named owner, a defined success metric, and a quarter of telemetry. Adoption target: **70%+ active use in target groups within 60 days** of rollout. *(single source — verify before publishing)*
- McKinsey 2025 data: 88% of firms use AI in at least one function, but **agentic scaling sits at 23%** enterprise-wide. Deloitte 2026: only **25% have moved 40%+ of experiments into production** (54% expected to).
- Top barriers: workforce adoption rate unknown (45.6%), inconsistent governance (37.1%), no value metrics (28.9%) — all three are change-management territory.
**Sources:** [Capably — AI adoption metrics](https://www.capably.ai/resources/measuring-ai-adoption-metrics), [GoGloby adoption statistics](https://gogloby.com/insights/ai-adoption-statistics/), [Larridin enterprise guide](https://larridin.com/solutions/ai-adoption-the-complete-enterprise-guide-2026)

## Regulatory clock: EU AI Act enforcement begins 2 August 2026

The majority of EU AI Act provisions become enforceable on **2 August 2026** — fines up to €35M or 7% of turnover. The **AI literacy obligation (Article 4)** has applied since February 2025: staff AI literacy is now a legal duty, which converts training and change management from a nice-to-have into a compliance line item. Every EU agentic rollout this year is a change program with a statutory deadline.
**Sources:** [is4.ai — EU AI Act compliance guide 2026](https://www.is4.ai/blog/our-blog-1/eu-ai-act-compliance-guide-2026-345), [PwC — EU AI Act](https://cee.pwc.com/eu-ai-act-compliance-and-transformation.html), [Deloitte — EU AI Act governance](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/services/consulting/articles/eu-ai-act-ai-governance.html)
