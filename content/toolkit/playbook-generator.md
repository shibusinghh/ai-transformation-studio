# Tool — AI Adoption Playbook Generator

**What it does:** takes a 12-question intake about an organization (size, EU footprint, what's being deployed, sponsor, prior AI attempts, restructuring history, works councils, candidate workflows, literacy state, governance today, success definition, change capacity) and generates a tailored end-to-end adoption playbook for a Copilot/Claude/agent rollout.

**Built on method, not templates:** every playbook is an adaptation of the six-move sequence from my Change Management Playbook for Agentic AI — inventory with amnesty, buy trust with real currency, redesign workflows not tasks, managers-first rollout, boring governance, dissolve the program. The intake answers cut, reorder, and resize the moves. Recent restructuring or silently killed pilots automatically harden the trust posture (opt-in cohorts, published guarantees) — even when that conflicts with the sponsor's timeline. Especially then.

**What every output contains:** wave plan with gates · guardrails adapted from the G1-G5 set · risk register with named tripwires · the four-number scorecard (workflows in production, active use at 60 days, literacy coverage, registry breaches) · comms cascade and manager enablement calendar · a "what makes it stick" close. EU organizations get the Article 4 literacy workstream with evidence logging.

**Benchmarks cited, never invented:** 70% active use in 60 days, the 2-4 owned-workflows baseline, the 79%/11% adoption gap — sourced. If the sponsor's targets beat the published benchmarks, the playbook flags the gap instead of adopting heroic numbers.

**The guarantee:** every playbook contains at least one recommendation the sponsor will not like. If it reads as pure validation, it gets rewritten.

**How to run:** open Claude Code in this repo and say "generate an adoption playbook" — it will ask the intake questions and write the document.
